Module 3: Requirements Engineering in the AI Era

Time: 11:15 AM – 12:15 PM | Duration: 45 min teaching + 15 min Q&A

Learning Objectives

  • Distinguish functional vs. non-functional requirements.
  • Elicit and validate requirements using modern tools (LLMs, Salesforce Einstein).
  • Avoid ambiguity and incompleteness in AI-driven systems.

🔍 Real-World Story: CRM → Salesforce Einstein

1990s – Traditional CRM: Users filled forms. Reports were static.

2016 – Salesforce Einstein: AI layer that predicts lead conversion, recommends next best action, and auto-generates summaries.

Requirement Shift: From “store customer data” to “predict customer churn with 90% accuracy and explain why.”

Challenge: How do you specify “explainable AI” in a requirement?

Requirements in SWEBOK V4

  • Functional: What the system does (e.g., “User can reset password”).
  • Non-functional: How well it does it (e.g., “Password reset email arrives in < 30 sec”).
  • Constraints: “Must comply with GDPR.”
  • LLM-Specific: “Cite sources,” “Refuse medical advice,” “Support 5 languages.”

Modern Tools & Pitfalls

  • LLM Prompt: “Generate user stories for a mental health chatbot that respects privacy and avoids diagnosis.”
  • Pitfall: “The system shall be intelligent” → too vague.
  • Fix: “The LLM shall refuse to answer medical questions and redirect to a human professional.”
  • Tool: Jira + AI plugins auto-tag requirements by risk and traceability.

🎯 Interactive Activity (10 min)

Fix This Requirement:

  1. Give students: “The AI shall be helpful.”
  2. In pairs, rewrite it using SMART + AI guardrails.
  3. Share best version with class.

❓ Q&A Discussion Prompts (15 min)

  1. How do you validate an LLM’s behavior before coding?
  2. Can non-functional requirements be more important than functional ones in AI systems?
  3. Who owns requirements in an AI project: data scientist, engineer, or product manager?
  4. Should LLMs be required to cite sources like Perplexity?
  5. How do you handle conflicting stakeholder needs (e.g., marketing wants personalization, legal wants privacy)?
